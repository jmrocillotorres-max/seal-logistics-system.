SEAL CORP | Incoterms Quick Guide

Incoterms (International Commercial Terms) define exactly who pays for what, and who assumes the risk during international shipping.

🏭 EXW (Ex Works)

Buyer assumes all risk and costs. The seller just makes the goods available at their warehouse. The buyer handles export clearance, freight, and import.

🚢 FOB (Free On Board)

Risk shifts at the departure port. The seller handles export customs and loading onto the ship. The buyer pays for the main sea freight and import.

🌊 CIF (Cost, Insurance & Freight)

Seller pays freight and insurance. The seller gets the goods to the destination port and buys insurance, but risk technically transfers when loaded on the ship.

🚚 DDP (Delivered Duty Paid)

Seller assumes all risk and costs. The seller delivers the goods directly to the buyer's door, fully cleared for import, including paying all duties and taxes.

📦 Shipping Methods Explained
LCL (Less than Container Load)

Best for smaller shipments. Your cargo shares container space with other shipments, meaning you only pay for the volume you use. Cost-effective but can take slightly longer due to consolidation.

FCL (Full Container Load)

You rent an entire 20ft or 40ft container exclusively for your goods. This is faster and more secure since the container is sealed at the origin and only opened when it reaches you.

Air Freight

The fastest shipping method available. Ideal for highly urgent, lightweight, or high-value cargo, though it is the most expensive option. Charged by weight (kg) or volume, whichever is higher.
